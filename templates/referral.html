Referral Notice

This page explains when an in-person consultation with a General Practitioner (GP), specialist, or emergency service is required. Online consultations have limitations and are not suitable for all situations.

1. When You Must See a Doctor in Person

You should seek an in-person medical assessment if you experience:

  • Persistent or worsening symptoms
  • Symptoms requiring physical examination
  • Abnormal findings that need investigation (e.g. scans, blood tests)
  • Conditions requiring hands-on treatment or procedures

2. GP Referral

In many cases, your General Practitioner is the most appropriate first point of care. A GP can examine you, arrange investigations, and refer you to a specialist if needed.

3. Specialist Referral

You may be advised to see a gynaecologist or another specialist when:

  • Your condition requires specialised assessment
  • Further diagnostic tests are necessary
  • Symptoms do not improve with initial management

4. Acute and Emergency Situations

Seek immediate medical care or attend the nearest emergency department if you have:
  • Severe or sudden abdominal or pelvic pain
  • Heavy vaginal bleeding
  • Fainting, collapse, or severe dizziness
  • Suspected ectopic pregnancy
  • Any rapidly worsening or alarming symptoms

5. Online Advice Limitations

Online advice is based solely on the information you provide and cannot replace physical examination or urgent care when required.

Your safety always comes first.
If in doubt, seek in-person medical care.

Referral & Escalation Notice

This online consultation service is designed to support women with non-urgent gynaecological concerns that are suitable for assessment and guidance via telemedicine.

There are circumstances in which escalation or referral to face-to-face care is necessary. These include, but are not limited to:

  • Symptoms suggestive of acute or severe illness
  • Conditions requiring physical examination or imaging
  • Situations where urgent hospital assessment is appropriate
  • Concerns falling outside the scope of telemedicine

Specialist and GP Referral

Where appropriate, I may recommend referral to:

  • Your General Practitioner (GP)
  • A hospital-based gynaecology service
  • Emergency or urgent care services

If required, a GP liaison letter can be provided to support continuity of care and appropriate onward management.

Personal note:
I strongly encourage patients to remain engaged with their GP alongside any specialist advice provided through this platform. Telemedicine works best when integrated with ongoing primary care.

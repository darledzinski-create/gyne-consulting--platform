Referral Guidance & Continuity of Care

Supporting safe, appropriate and coordinated medical care

Purpose of This Service

This online gynaecological consultation service is designed to provide medical advice, assessment, explanation and guidance based on the information you choose to share.

It is intended to support — not replace — face-to-face medical care.

Your Ongoing Medical Care

Your General Practitioner (GP) or usual healthcare provider remains central to your long-term care.

In some situations, online consultation alone may not be sufficient. When this is the case, you may be advised to seek further care.

When GP Review Is Recommended

You may be advised to contact your GP if:

  • Your symptoms persist, worsen, or recur
  • A physical examination is required
  • Monitoring or follow-up is necessary
  • Investigations such as blood tests or imaging are appropriate
  • A prescription requires local supervision

When Specialist Referral Is Advised

Specialist referral may be recommended when:

  • There are warning signs (“red flags”)
  • The diagnosis remains uncertain
  • Symptoms do not respond to initial advice
  • Further specialist expertise is required

If specialist referral is advised, you will be encouraged to arrange this through your GP or local healthcare system.

Patient Responsibility

By using this service, you understand that:

  • You may be advised to seek further medical care
  • It is your responsibility to act on referral advice given
  • You may share consultation information with your GP if appropriate
Important: Online consultations work best when they are part of coordinated, ongoing medical care.

Emergency & Acute Matters

This service does not provide emergency care.

If you experience acute symptoms — such as severe pain, heavy bleeding, collapse, chest pain, or sudden deterioration — you must seek immediate local medical attention.

Continuity of Care Statement

This platform aims to complement existing healthcare, promote understanding, and support informed decision-making.

Good medicine depends on continuity, communication, and collaboration — this service is one part of that process.

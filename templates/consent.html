Telemedicine Consent

This Telemedicine Consent outlines the nature, limitations, benefits, and risks of remote gynaecological consultation. By continuing to use this platform, you confirm your understanding and acceptance of the following:

1. Nature of Telemedicine

Telemedicine enables a medical consultation without a physical face-to-face examination. Advice is based solely on the information you provide.

2. Limitations

Without a physical examination or diagnostic tests, some conditions cannot be accurately diagnosed or treated. You accept that recommendations may be limited compared to an in-person assessment. Learn what to expect from your initial assessment in our First Consultation Packagea>

3. Benefits

• Efficient access to medical advice • Private and confidential communication • No need for travel or waiting rooms

4. Risks

• Technical failures (internet, email, platform availability) • Misinterpretation of symptoms due to incomplete information • The possibility that telemedicine may not be appropriate for your condition

5. Acute Medical Conditions

Telemedicine is not suitable for acute or emergency gynaecological problems. If you experience severe pain, heavy bleeding, fever, or any rapidly worsening symptoms, you must seek in-person medical care or attend the nearest emergency facility.

6. Confidentiality

All information you submit is handled with strict confidentiality. Electronic communication, while safeguarded, may still carry minimal security risks inherent to digital communication.

7. Your Consent

By submitting a question or using this service, you confirm that: • You are over 18 years old • You understand the limitations of telemedicine • You voluntarily consent to receive care through this platform • You accept personal responsibility for the accuracy of the information you provide

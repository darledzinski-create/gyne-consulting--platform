Prescription Policy

This page explains how prescriptions are handled on the Gynae Consulting Platform, and what patients can reasonably expect from an online consultation.

General Principles

Prescriptions are issued only when, in my professional judgement, it is safe, appropriate, and clinically justified to do so. An online consultation does not automatically guarantee that a prescription will be provided.

When Prescriptions May Be Issued

A prescription may be considered when:

  • A clear and sufficient medical history has been provided
  • The condition is suitable for remote assessment
  • No physical examination is required for safe prescribing
  • The medication is appropriate for remote initiation or continuation

When Prescriptions Cannot Be Issued

Prescriptions will not be issued when:

  • The condition requires physical examination or investigations
  • The clinical information provided is incomplete or unclear
  • The medication requested is unsafe without face-to-face assessment
  • The request falls outside accepted medical or regulatory guidelines

Controlled and Restricted Medications

Controlled drugs, addictive medications, and certain high-risk treatments cannot be prescribed through this platform. Patients requesting such medications will be advised to consult their local GP or specialist.

Follow-Up and Ongoing Care

Some prescriptions may require follow-up consultations or coordination with your GP. Long-term prescribing is normally managed by your primary care provider.

Please note:
Your safety is always the priority. If prescribing is not appropriate, clear explanations and alternative recommendations will be provided.

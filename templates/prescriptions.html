Prescriptions & Medical Documents Policy

This page explains what types of prescriptions and medical documents can and cannot be issued through online consultations.

1. General Principles

Any prescription or document issued following an online consultation is based solely on the information you provide. Online consultations have limitations and may not be suitable for all prescribing decisions.

2. Prescriptions

Prescriptions may be issued only when it is clinically safe and appropriate to do so without a physical examination.

Prescriptions may be declined if:

  • A physical examination is required
  • Further investigations are needed
  • The medication requested is not suitable for online prescribing
  • The information provided is insufficient
Certain medications, including controlled or high-risk drugs, cannot be prescribed through this platform.

3. Sick Notes and Medical Certificates

Sick notes or medical certificates may be issued only when clinically justified and supported by the information provided during the consultation.

Requests for certificates may be declined if:

  • The condition cannot be adequately assessed online
  • The request does not meet medical or ethical standards

4. Referral Letters

Referral letters to a GP or specialist may be provided when appropriate. These are intended to support further in-person assessment and care.

5. No Guarantee

Submitting a consultation request does not guarantee that a prescription, sick note, or medical document will be issued.

Your health and safety are the priority.
If in doubt, in-person medical assessment is recommended.

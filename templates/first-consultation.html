Online gynaecological consultations can be very helpful for many concerns. However, there are situations where a face-to-face medical assessment, urgent care, or referral to another healthcare professional is necessary to ensure your safety and wellbeing.

When online consultation may not be sufficient

You may be advised to seek in-person medical care, urgent assessment, or referral if:

  • Your symptoms suggest an acute or potentially serious medical condition
  • A physical examination is required to make a safe diagnosis
  • Investigations such as blood tests, scans, or swabs are necessary
  • Your symptoms worsen or do not improve as expected
  • Your concern falls outside the scope of safe online management

If at any point your symptoms are severe, rapidly worsening, or you are concerned about your immediate health, you should seek urgent medical attention through local emergency services or your nearest emergency department.

Specialist and GP referral

Where appropriate, you may be advised to consult:

  • Your General Practitioner (GP)
  • A local gynaecologist or relevant medical specialist
  • Emergency medical services

This recommendation is always made in your best interest and does not mean your concern is unimportant — only that it requires a different level of care.

Your role in safe care

By using this platform, you acknowledge that:

  • You will follow advice regarding escalation or referral when recommended
  • You will seek urgent care when symptoms are severe or concerning
  • You understand that online consultations have clinical limitations

Our shared goal is safe, respectful, and appropriate care — always placing your health first.

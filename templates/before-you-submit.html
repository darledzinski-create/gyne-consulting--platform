Before You Submit Your Question

This online consultation service is designed to provide thoughtful, professional medical guidance in a calm and confidential setting. To help you receive the most useful response, please take a moment to read the information below before submitting your question.

What this service is best suited for

  • Non-urgent gynaecological concerns
  • Clarification of symptoms, test results, or diagnoses
  • Discussion of treatment options or next steps
  • Second opinions or reassurance
  • Guidance on whether further examination or referral may be required

What this service cannot provide

  • Emergency or urgent medical care
  • Immediate diagnosis without appropriate examination or investigations
  • Care for acute or rapidly worsening symptoms

If you are experiencing acute pain, heavy bleeding, fever, fainting, or any other concerning symptoms, please seek immediate medical attention from local emergency services or your nearest healthcare provider.

How to write your question

You are encouraged to describe your concern in your own words. Helpful details may include:

  • Your age
  • Relevant medical or gynaecological history
  • Current symptoms and how long they have been present
  • Any treatments or medications you are using
  • What specifically you would like guidance or clarification on

There is no need to worry about medical terminology — clarity and honesty are far more important.

What happens after you submit

Once your question is received, it will be reviewed carefully. You may receive clarifying questions before advice is provided, as this helps ensure that any guidance offered is appropriate and safe.

Please note: This service is advisory in nature and complements — but does not replace — care provided by your own GP or specialist.

By submitting your question, you confirm that you have read and understood this information.
